This entire journey (and website) is one big experiment for me -- an exercise in being more open, honest, and vulnerable. With that comes the acceptance of all my imperfections, those things I spent years hiding, fixing, or even lying about.

**Why live like that anymore? I'm tired of being anyone but myself.**

This is both a personal and business project -- a space where I explore my thoughts, document my experiences, and share insights from my life.

### This site is for me first. This is my *[magnum opus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnum_opus_(alchemy))* or my life's "great work".

Writing has always been a way for me to process ideas, be grateful for moments in my life, and reflect on my journey.

I see this as an experimental performance project, an open-source approach to my life where I document not just what I experience but how I handle it. This idea has been on my mind for many years, but in practice, I've been actively writing and refining it for the past couple of years.

A turning point for me was realizing that, despite checking all the boxes of success -- education, career, societal expectations -- I still felt unfulfilled. That sense of being lost led me to rethink my direction and purpose. I'd always been in roles that involved helping others, but I struggled to see the direct impact. This project is my way of finding a different kind of impact for me: one that grows over time.

At its heart, my goal is simple: **to be open, honest, and vulnerable** -- as much as I can be. I know I can't be 100% perfectly transparent, but I aim for that. Life isn't perfect, and neither am I, but I believe there's value in sharing the process rather than just the results.

For more technical details about how the site was built, check my [sitemap](/sitemap/).

I hope you find something here that's meaningful to you. Feel free to reach out.

#### - Kyle (OpenSourceKyle)

# Some of My Influences

There are certain men who have greatly influenced my thinking and philosophies, driving me to take significant action in my life. I will caveat that I typically follow ideas in the space of **male self-improvement**, but with a focus on **action and positivity**, and less on the toxicity and negativity that can be rampant in that realm. These ideas are not necessarily mainstream, but they have undoubtedly helped me, my friends, and my clients. With so much positive impact, I cannot help but share them. Their ideas will need to be tailored to your own context, but I am sure they will help -- as they have greatly helped me.

Of note, they typically communicate in a very male way. Males and females biologically and socially communicate differently, but the details why are irrelevant here. Listen to the ideas. For me, some of these men have grown as their ideas have matured as well, and this serves as inspiration for myself to continue growing.

For me, the key is to listen to the parts that add a lot of value and not to get caught up in opinions on other things -- such as politics or personal beliefs. I have followed parts of each of these to help build the life that I have wanted for so long. I will briefly highlight what I appreciate about each of them.

## Andy of KillYourInnerLoser

<https://killyourinnerloser.com>

Specializing in BDSM, dating, and goals, Andy has been my #1 influence for the last few years. I initially read and used his ideas to strengthen my former marriage in connection and BDSM. I continue to apply those to this day in my new relationships and have added more of his positive mindset techniques to my life.

## Dr. Robert Glover of the book: *No More Mr. Nice Guy*

<https://www.drglover.com/no-more-mr-nice-guy/the-book.html>

Dr. Glover's book highlights the most important concept and issue of "nice guys": covert contracts, which he defines as essentially uncommunicated needs and desires with all the toxic expectations that come with them. I have listened to the audio version of this once, read the book twice, and completed all 40-something reflection exercises to realize that I was scared of communicating my needs and desires in every facet of my life. I have recommended this book to numerous friends and clients as well.

## Alex of Playing With Fire

<https://www.playingfire.com>

Alex specializes in online dating, specifically with phone swiping apps like Tinder. I find a lot of value in how to communicate through text messages for dates, but I have learned that it can be extrapolated to communication in other areas of my life.

## Austen Summers of the same business name

<https://www.austensummers.com>

Austen specializes in face-to-face communication and building authentic connections with women outside of the apps.

## Caleb Jones of the same business name

<https://calebjones.com>

Caleb offers a lot of content, but I personally have gotten a lot of value from his open relationship ideas and how to live internationally (though I discovered him after moving to Mexico). He provides very specific niche content for location-independent businesses as well.
